Explore Alberta's Rocky Mountain Parks through hiking adventures

The majestic Rocky Mountains of Alberta are home to three magnificent National Parks; Jasper, Banff, and Waterton. All three are full of opportunites to explore and enjoy the wonders of nature. My family particularly likes camping and hiking in these parks, where we have had many oppourtunities to enjoy to places where we can enjoy beautiful views and experiences.

Jasper

Nestled in the heart of Jasper National Park, the townsite of Jasper offers a charming escape surrounded by majestic mountain peaks. Originally established as a railway town in the early 1900s, Jasper boasts a rich history and serves as a hub for outdoor adventure.

Visitors can explore the town's cozy cafes, boutique shops, and cultural attractions, all set against the backdrop of pristine wilderness. From hiking and wildlife viewing to relaxing in natural hot springs, Jasper offers a wealth of activities for nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts alike.

Banff

The town of Banff is a charming mountain destination steeped in history and surrounded by stunning natural beauty. Established in 1885 as Canada's first national park, Banff offers visitors a mix of outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, and breathtaking scenery.

Explore the vibrant streets lined with boutiques, galleries, and eateries, or take in the views from the iconic Banff Avenue. Outdoor enthusiasts can enjoy hiking, skiing, and wildlife spotting in the surrounding park, while relaxation seekers can unwind in natural hot springs or indulge in spa treatments.

Waterton

Located in the heart of Waterton Lakes National Park, the townsite of Waterton is a picturesque mountain village renowned for its stunning scenery and abundant wildlife. Set against the backdrop of rugged peaks and pristine lakes, Waterton offers visitors a tranquil escape in the Canadian Rockies.

Explore the charming streets lined with historic buildings, quaint shops, and inviting cafes, or embark on outdoor adventures such as hiking, boating, and wildlife watching in the surrounding park. Don't miss iconic attractions like the Prince of Wales Hotel and the scenic Red Rock Canyon.
